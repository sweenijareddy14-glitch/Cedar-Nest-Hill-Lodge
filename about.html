Our Story

A lodge built by a family who never left the mountains

Cedar Nest started as a four-room guesthouse in 2014. Twelve years on, it's still run by the same family — just with a few more cedar trees planted around it.

Who we are

Cedar Nest Hill Lodge is owned and run by the Thakur family, who have lived on this hillside above Old Manali for three generations. What began as spare rooms rented to trekkers has grown into a 14-room budget lodge — but the goal hasn't changed: give travellers a clean, safe, affordable place to rest between treks.

We're not a resort, and we don't try to be. There's no spa, no rooftop infinity pool — just honest rooms, real food, and people who know every trail within twenty kilometres.

Our promise

Every rupee you pay goes toward keeping the lodge clean, the water hot, and the kitchen stocked — not toward a marketing budget. We keep prices low by keeping things simple, and we make up for the lack of frills with genuine hospitality.

If you need a packed lunch for a sunrise trek, a recommendation for a quieter trail, or just someone to share a cup of chai with by the bonfire, you'll find that here.
